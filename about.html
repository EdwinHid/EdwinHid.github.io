About

Isue Shin is a filmmaker creating work relating to identity and memory. She is currently based in Los Angeles.

Isue received a BA in Modern Culture and Media from Brown University in 2017. There, she was part of Brown Motion Pictures, a student-run production company where she worked on numerous projects. As a cinematographer, her web series Real World Experience (2015) was shown at the Los Angeles Independent Film Festival and her short film Look (2015) played at the Oak Cliff Film Festival. Additionally, she made music videos, promotional videos, video essays, and short form documentaries during her time at Brown. She created a photo zine (as) seen by you and me (2017) with Ying Cai and her essay America, Dad, Dad, America appeared in Visions Magazine in 2016.

During the past year, Isue worked under New Moon Pictures as Director’s Assistant to Pamela Tom in her upcoming PBS documentary. She is currently working as an AC under Cinematographer Christopher Ripley.
